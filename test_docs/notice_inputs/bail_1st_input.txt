Title: 1st Bail Application of Pappu Nayak under Section 483 BNSS

Court Details: High Court of Madhya Pradesh, Bench at Gwalior. M.Cr.C. No. 21590/2026.

Applicant Details:
Pappu Nayak, S/o Shri Mansha Nayak, aged 40 years, R/o Village Sahapura Colony, Tehsil Shamshabad, District Vidisha (M.P.). Currently in judicial custody since 30/04/2026.

Opposite Party / State:
The State of Madhya Pradesh, through Police Station Shamshabad, District Vidisha (M.P.).

FIR / Crime Details:
Crime No. 158/2026, Police Station Shamshabad, District Vidisha (M.P.). Offence under Sections 64(1) and 332(6) of the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023. Date of arrest: 30/04/2026.

Impugned Order:
Bail Application No. 126/2026 BA dismissed by the IInd Additional Sessions Judge, GanjBasoda, District Vidisha (M.P.), vide order dated 05/05/2026.

Bail History:
This is the applicant's first bail application before the High Court. No bail application pending before or decided by the Hon'ble Supreme Court of India or this Hon'ble High Court. Earlier subordinate-court application B.A. No. 126/26 dated 05/05/2026 was dismissed (impugned).

Criminal History:
Nil — applicant has no prior criminal antecedents.

Co-accused / Cross case:
Nil — sole accused; no cross case.

Facts of the case:
As per the prosecution story, on 28/04/2026 at about 7:30 p.m. there was an altercation between the children of the applicant Pappu Nayak and the children of the prosecutrix (his neighbour). About one and a half hours later, the applicant is alleged to have come home drunk and abused the prosecutrix, who reported the matter at Satpada Police Station. On 29/04/2026 at around 01:30 a.m., while the prosecutrix was sleeping in the courtyard outside (her husband being away at DJ work), she claims the applicant entered the courtyard and committed the alleged offence; her 13-year-old daughter Anjali is said to have woken up upon her scream, and the applicant ran away. The prosecutrix narrated the incident to her husband at 9:00 a.m. on his return and lodged the FIR at Police Station Shamshabad. Crime No. 158/2026 was registered under Sections 64(1) and 332(6) of the BNS.

Grounds / Defence:
1. The applicant is innocent and falsely implicated; he has no concern with the alleged offence and the learned court below has illegally rejected his bail.
2. The applicant is a law-abiding, peace-loving citizen of India and there is no evidence against him.
3. A dispute had arisen between the families of the complainant and the applicant over a fight between their children; the complainant assaulted the applicant and his children, and on the applicant's wife proposing to file a report, the complainant went to the police station and falsely implicated the applicant.
4. The applicant is a married father of 5 children; the complainant is also the married mother of 4 children. The complainant has no external or internal injuries.
5. The applicant is in custody since 30/04/2026, is a poor person and the sole earning member of his family; prolonged custody will affect his family badly.
6. The applicant has no criminal history and has been falsely implicated; he deserves to be enlarged on bail in the interest of justice.
7. Other grounds shall be urged at the time of final hearing.

Local Residence & Surety:
The applicant is a local resident of Sahapura Colony, Tehsil Shamshabad, District Vidisha (M.P.); there is no apprehension that he will abscond or tamper with the prosecution case. The applicant is ready to furnish adequate surety and abide by any conditions imposed by this Hon'ble Court.

Relief Sought:
That the applicant be enlarged on bail in Crime No. 158/2026, Police Station Shamshabad, District Vidisha (M.P.).
