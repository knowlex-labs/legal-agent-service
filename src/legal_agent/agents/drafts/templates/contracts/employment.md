# EMPLOYMENT AGREEMENT

**THIS EMPLOYMENT AGREEMENT** (hereinafter referred to as **"this Agreement"**) is made and executed at Mumbai on this 1st day of May, 2026.

**BETWEEN:**

**EXAMPLE TECH PRIVATE LIMITED**, a company incorporated under the Companies Act, 2013, bearing CIN: U72900MH2020PTC123456 and PAN: AABCE1234F, having its registered office at 801, Example Towers, Bandra-Kurla Complex, Mumbai — 400 051, Maharashtra, India (hereinafter referred to as the **"Employer"** or **"Company"**, which expression shall, unless repugnant to the context or meaning thereof, be deemed to include its successors-in-interest and permitted assigns) of the **FIRST PART**;

**AND**

**Ms. Priya Example**, aged 28 years, daughter of Mr. Rajesh Example, holding PAN: ABCDE5678G, residing at 12A, Example Apartments, Andheri West, Mumbai — 400 058, Maharashtra, India (hereinafter referred to as the **"Employee"**, which expression shall, unless repugnant to the context or meaning thereof, be deemed to include her heirs, executors, administrators, and legal representatives) of the **SECOND PART**.

The Employer and the Employee are hereinafter individually referred to as a **"Party"** and collectively as the **"Parties"**.

---

## RECITALS

**WHEREAS** the Employer is engaged in the business of software product development and provides technology services to clients in India and overseas;

**WHEREAS** the Employee represents that she possesses the qualifications, skills, experience, and aptitude required to perform the duties of the position offered by the Employer;

**WHEREAS** the Parties desire to record the terms and conditions governing the Employee's employment with the Employer.

**NOW, THEREFORE**, in consideration of the mutual covenants and promises contained herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties agree as follows:

---

## 1. APPOINTMENT AND POSITION

1.1 The Employer hereby appoints the Employee, and the Employee hereby accepts the appointment, as **Senior Software Engineer** in the Engineering division of the Employer.

1.2 The Employee shall report to the Vice President — Engineering, or such other person as the Employer may designate from time to time in writing.

1.3 The Employee shall perform the duties described in the Job Description annexed hereto as **Schedule C**, and such other duties consistent with her position as the Employer may reasonably assign from time to time.

1.4 The Employee undertakes to devote her full working time, attention, and abilities to the business of the Employer and to faithfully serve the Employer during the term of this Agreement.

---

## 2. COMMENCEMENT, PROBATION AND TERM

2.1 **Start Date.** The Employee's employment shall commence on **1st May, 2026** (the **"Start Date"**).

2.2 **Probation.** The Employee shall be on probation for an initial period of **six (6) months** from the Start Date. During probation, either Party may terminate this Agreement by giving the other thirty (30) days' prior written notice or salary in lieu thereof.

2.3 **Confirmation.** On satisfactory completion of probation, the Employer shall confirm the Employee's employment in writing. If no communication is issued at the end of the probation period, probation shall be deemed extended by one (1) further month, after which employment shall be deemed confirmed.

2.4 **Term.** Subject to earlier termination in accordance with Clause 12, this Agreement shall continue until terminated by either Party as provided herein.

---

## 3. PLACE OF WORK AND WORKING HOURS

3.1 **Place of Work.** The Employee's primary place of work shall be the Employer's office at Bandra-Kurla Complex, Mumbai. The Employer reserves the right to require the Employee to work from any other location in India in accordance with reasonable business requirements, subject to reasonable prior notice.

3.2 **Remote Work.** The Employer operates a hybrid working policy. The Employee may work remotely for up to two (2) working days per week, subject to the Employer's prevailing remote-work policy.

3.3 **Working Hours.** The Employee's standard working hours shall be 9:30 AM to 6:30 PM, Monday to Friday, with a one-hour lunch break. The nature of the role may require additional hours from time to time, for which no separate remuneration shall be payable.

---

## 4. REMUNERATION AND CTC

4.1 **Cost to Company (CTC).** The Employee's total cost-to-company shall be **Rs. 24,00,000/- (Rupees Twenty-Four Lakh Only)** per annum, inclusive of all statutory contributions by the Employer. The detailed salary breakup is set out in **Schedule A**.

4.2 **Payment Schedule.** Salary shall be payable monthly, in arrears, on or before the last working day of each calendar month, by direct credit to the Employee's designated bank account.

4.3 **Tax Deductions.** The Employer shall deduct tax at source under §192 of the Income Tax Act, 1961 and remit the same to the appropriate authority. The Employer shall issue Form 16 within the period prescribed by law.

4.4 **Annual Review.** The Employee's compensation shall be reviewed annually, in line with the Employer's performance-appraisal cycle. Any revision shall be at the sole discretion of the Employer and shall be recorded by way of a written amendment to this Agreement.

4.5 **Variable Pay.** The Employee shall be eligible for performance-linked variable pay as detailed in **Schedule A**, payable annually subject to the Employer's performance-appraisal outcomes and prevailing variable-pay policy.

---

## 5. STATUTORY BENEFITS

5.1 **Provident Fund.** The Employee shall be enrolled under the Employees' Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952. The Employer and the Employee shall each contribute 12% of the Employee's Basic salary to the Employees' Provident Fund, as required by the EPF Act.

5.2 **ESI.** To the extent the Employee is covered under the Employees' State Insurance Act, 1948 having regard to wage thresholds, the Employer shall make the applicable contributions. As of the Start Date, the Employee's wages place her above the ESI ceiling and she is not covered by ESI.

5.3 **Gratuity.** On completion of five (5) years of continuous service with the Employer, the Employee shall be entitled to gratuity under the Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972 at the rate of fifteen (15) days' Basic salary for each completed year of service.

5.4 **Group Medical Insurance.** The Employee, her spouse, and up to two (2) dependent children shall be covered under the Employer's group mediclaim policy, with a sum insured of Rs. 5,00,000/- per annum per family, subject to the terms of the policy.

5.5 **Group Term Life Insurance.** The Employee shall be covered under a group term life insurance policy for a sum assured of Rs. 50,00,000/- (Rupees Fifty Lakh Only), subject to the terms of the policy.

---

## 6. LEAVE POLICY

6.1 The Employee shall be entitled to paid leave in accordance with **Schedule B** of this Agreement and the Employer's prevailing leave policy. In the event of conflict between Schedule B and the leave policy, Schedule B shall prevail.

6.2 **Accrual and Carry-forward.** Leave shall accrue on a pro-rata basis during the Employee's first year of service. Unavailed earned leave may be carried forward up to a maximum of thirty (30) days; any excess shall lapse at the end of the calendar year.

6.3 **Encashment.** Unavailed earned leave, up to the carry-forward cap, shall be encashable on separation, calculated on Basic salary.

6.4 **Maternity Leave.** The Employee shall be entitled to maternity leave as per the Maternity Benefit Act, 1961 (as amended in 2017), i.e., twenty-six (26) weeks of paid leave for the first two children.

---

## 7. POSH ACT COMPLIANCE

7.1 The Employer has constituted an Internal Committee under §4 of the Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013 (**"PoSH Act"**) to receive and investigate complaints of sexual harassment.

7.2 The Employer has a Policy on Prevention of Sexual Harassment at the Workplace (the **"PoSH Policy"**), a copy of which has been made available to the Employee. The Employee acknowledges receipt of the PoSH Policy and undertakes to comply with it.

7.3 The Employee shall attend such sensitisation and training sessions on prevention of sexual harassment as the Employer may organise from time to time, in compliance with §19 of the PoSH Act.

---

## 8. CONFIDENTIALITY

8.1 The Employee shall, both during and after the term of her employment with the Employer, hold in strict confidence all Confidential Information of the Employer and shall not disclose, use, or reproduce the same except as strictly required in the performance of her duties.

8.2 **"Confidential Information"** shall mean all information of a commercial, technical, operational, financial, or proprietary nature relating to the Employer, its clients, vendors, and employees, including source code, architecture documents, customer lists, pricing, salary information, and any information marked as confidential or that reasonably ought to be treated as confidential given its nature and the circumstances of disclosure.

8.3 **Exceptions.** The obligations in this Clause 8 shall not apply to information that (a) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Employee; (b) was rightfully in the Employee's possession prior to her employment, as evidenced by contemporaneous records; or (c) is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, or court order, provided the Employee gives the Employer prompt written notice (to the extent legally permissible) and cooperates in seeking a protective order.

8.4 On termination of employment, the Employee shall promptly return or destroy all Confidential Information in her possession in accordance with Clause 13.

---

## 9. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

9.1 **Work Product.** All inventions, discoveries, software, code, designs, documentation, process improvements, and other work product created by the Employee (a) during the course of her employment, (b) using the Employer's resources, or (c) relating to the Employer's business, shall be the sole and exclusive property of the Employer. The Employee hereby irrevocably assigns all right, title, and interest in such work product to the Employer.

9.2 **Moral Rights.** To the extent permitted by law, the Employee waives all moral rights in the Work Product.

9.3 **Pre-existing IP.** The Employee represents that she has disclosed to the Employer in writing a list of all Intellectual Property owned by her prior to the Start Date (**"Pre-existing IP"**). Pre-existing IP not listed shall be deemed not to exist. The Employee shall not incorporate any Pre-existing IP into the Work Product without prior written consent of the Employer; where consent is given, the Employee grants the Employer a perpetual, worldwide, royalty-free, sublicensable licence to use such Pre-existing IP for the purposes of the Employer's business.

9.4 **Further Assurances.** The Employee shall, at the Employer's cost, execute all documents and do all acts reasonably necessary to perfect the Employer's rights in the Work Product, including filing patent applications, copyright assignments, and trademark registrations.

---

## 10. NON-SOLICITATION AND NON-COMPETE

10.1 **Non-Solicitation of Employees.** For a period of twelve (12) months following termination of this Agreement (howsoever caused), the Employee shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit, induce, or attempt to induce any employee of the Employer to leave his or her employment with the Employer.

10.2 **Non-Solicitation of Clients.** For a period of twelve (12) months following termination of this Agreement, the Employee shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit or accept business from any client, customer, or prospect of the Employer with whom the Employee had material contact during the twelve (12) months preceding termination, in respect of services competitive with those provided by the Employer.

10.3 **Non-Compete.** For a period of twelve (12) months following termination of this Agreement, the Employee shall not, directly or indirectly, engage in, own, manage, operate, or participate in the ownership, management, or operation of any business in India that is in direct competition with the core business of the Employer. The Parties acknowledge that this covenant is intended to protect the Employer's legitimate business interests, including confidential information and customer goodwill, and is drafted narrowly in scope and duration consistent with §27 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872.

10.4 **Reasonableness.** The Parties acknowledge that the restrictions in this Clause 10 are reasonable in scope, duration, and geographic area, having regard to the nature of the Employer's business. If any restriction is held by a court of competent jurisdiction to be unenforceable, it shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it enforceable, and the remaining restrictions shall continue to apply.

---

## 11. DATA PROTECTION

11.1 The Employee acknowledges that the Employer shall process her personal data (including identity, contact, financial, and statutory information) for purposes related to her employment, payroll, statutory compliance, and performance management.

11.2 The Employer shall process such personal data in accordance with the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, with purpose limitation, data-minimisation, and secure-processing principles observed.

11.3 The Employee shall, in the course of her duties, handle personal data of clients, fellow employees, and third parties. The Employee shall process such data only as authorised and shall report any personal-data breach to the Employer within twenty-four (24) hours of becoming aware of it.

---

## 12. TERMINATION

12.1 **Termination for Convenience.** After confirmation of employment, either Party may terminate this Agreement by giving the other **ninety (90) days'** prior written notice or salary in lieu thereof.

12.2 **Termination for Cause.** Notwithstanding Clause 12.1, the Employer may terminate this Agreement summarily, without notice or payment in lieu, in the event of:

(a) proven misconduct, including dishonesty, fraud, theft, or misappropriation of the Employer's or any third party's property;

(b) wilful disobedience of lawful and reasonable instructions;

(c) conviction for any offence involving moral turpitude;

(d) unauthorised absence from work for a continuous period exceeding seven (7) working days;

(e) intoxication at the workplace, harassment (including under the PoSH Act), or gross insubordination;

(f) material breach of this Agreement, the Employer's code of conduct, or any applicable law or regulation.

12.3 **Resignation.** If the Employee wishes to resign, she shall give the Employer ninety (90) days' prior written notice. The Employer may, at its discretion, waive all or part of the notice period or require payment of salary in lieu of unserved notice.

12.4 **Garden Leave.** During the notice period, the Employer may, at its discretion, place the Employee on garden leave, during which the Employee shall remain employed, shall continue to receive salary and benefits, but shall not be required to attend work or communicate with clients, colleagues, or suppliers.

---

## 13. RETURN OF PROPERTY

13.1 On termination of employment for any reason, or earlier on demand by the Employer, the Employee shall immediately return to the Employer all property of the Employer in her possession, custody, or control, including:

(a) laptops, mobile devices, access cards, identity cards, keys, and any other physical equipment;

(b) all documents, files, records, notebooks, presentations, client lists, and correspondence (whether in hard copy or electronic form);

(c) all Confidential Information, including any copies, extracts, or summaries thereof;

(d) all login credentials, passwords, and access tokens for the Employer's systems and third-party services.

13.2 The Employee shall certify compliance with this Clause 13 in writing, in the form required by the Employer, as a condition precedent to release of her full and final settlement.

---

## 14. INDEMNIFICATION

14.1 The Employee shall indemnify and keep the Employer indemnified from and against all losses, costs, damages, and expenses (including reasonable legal fees) arising out of or in connection with (a) any wilful misconduct, gross negligence, or fraud by the Employee in the performance of her duties; (b) any breach of this Agreement by the Employee; or (c) any claim by a third party arising out of an act or omission by the Employee outside the scope of her authorised duties.

14.2 The Employer shall indemnify the Employee in respect of any claim by a third party arising out of the Employee's performance of her duties within the scope of her authority and in accordance with the Employer's instructions, save to the extent such claim results from the Employee's wilful misconduct, gross negligence, or fraud.

---

## 15. GOVERNING LAW AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION

15.1 **Governing Law.** This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the Republic of India.

15.2 **Amicable Resolution.** Any dispute, controversy, or claim arising out of or in connection with this Agreement (a **"Dispute"**) shall, in the first instance, be sought to be resolved amicably between the Parties within thirty (30) days of written notice by the aggrieved Party.

15.3 **Arbitration.** If the Dispute is not resolved under Clause 15.2 within the stipulated period, it shall be finally settled by arbitration under the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 (as amended), by a sole arbitrator mutually appointed by the Parties, failing which appointed in accordance with §11 of the said Act. The **seat of arbitration** shall be Mumbai, India — the registered-office city of the Employer — which determines supervisory jurisdiction under §§9, 11, and 34 of the Act. The proceedings shall be in English. The award of the arbitrator shall be final and binding on the Parties.

15.4 **Interim Relief.** Notwithstanding Clause 15.3, either Party may approach the competent courts at Mumbai for urgent interim or injunctive relief, pending constitution of the arbitral tribunal.

---

## 16. SURVIVAL

16.1 Clauses 8 (Confidentiality), 9 (Intellectual Property), 10 (Non-Solicitation and Non-Compete), 11 (Data Protection), 13 (Return of Property), 14 (Indemnification), and 15 (Governing Law and Dispute Resolution) shall survive the termination or expiration of this Agreement.

---

## 17. MISCELLANEOUS

17.1 **Notices.** All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and delivered by hand, registered post with acknowledgement due, or email with read receipt, to the addresses first set out above, or to such other address as a Party may notify the other in writing.

17.2 **Entire Agreement.** This Agreement, together with its Schedules, constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to its subject matter and supersedes all prior oral or written communications, offers, or representations.

17.3 **Amendment.** No amendment to this Agreement shall be valid or binding unless made in writing and signed by both Parties.

17.4 **Severability.** If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid or unenforceable by a court of competent jurisdiction, such provision shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it valid and enforceable, and the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

17.5 **Waiver.** No failure or delay by a Party in exercising any right under this Agreement shall operate as a waiver thereof.

17.6 **Assignment.** The Employee shall not assign or delegate any of her rights or obligations under this Agreement. The Employer may assign this Agreement to a successor-in-interest on a merger, acquisition, or sale of all or substantially all of its business.

17.7 **Counterparts and Electronic Execution.** This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original, and electronic signatures shall be deemed valid under the Information Technology Act, 2000.

17.8 **Stamp Duty.** This Agreement shall be stamped in accordance with the Maharashtra Stamp Act, 1958. The stamp duty and any incidental expenses shall be borne by the Employer.

## SCHEDULE C — JOB DESCRIPTION

**Role:** Senior Software Engineer, Engineering Division

**Key Responsibilities:**

1. Design, develop, test, and maintain production software systems in the Employer's technology stack.
2. Conduct code reviews and mentor junior members of the engineering team.
3. Collaborate with product management and design to translate requirements into technical specifications.
4. Participate in on-call rotations to ensure reliability and availability of production services.
5. Contribute to architectural decisions and technology selection for new initiatives.
6. Ensure adherence to the Employer's security, data protection, and engineering-quality standards.

**Qualifications:**

1. Bachelor's or Master's degree in Computer Science, Engineering, or related discipline.
2. Minimum four (4) years of professional software development experience.
3. Proficiency in one or more modern programming languages (Python, Go, Java, or TypeScript).
4. Familiarity with cloud platforms (AWS, GCP, or Azure) and modern software-delivery practices.
